How to catch a thief – Birbal and a straw

Study the vocabulary first.

  • Conclude [kənˈkluːd] – сделать вывод
  • Equal [ˈiːkw(ə)l] – равный
  • Length [leŋθ] – длина
  • Mumble [ˈmʌmbl] – бубнить
  • Rob [rɒb] – грабить
  • Robbery [ˈrɒb(ə)rɪ] – ограбление
  • Separate [ˈseprət] – отдельный
  • Shorten [ˈʃɔːt(ə)n] – укоротить
  • Steal [stiːl] – воровать
  • Straw [strɔː] – соломинка
  • Vanish [ˈvænɪʃ] – исчезать
  • Thief [θiːf] – вор

Read the text an answer the questions.

Once a rich man who lived in Deli was robbed. He woke up one morning and saw that the gold had vanished. After some time, the man concluded that only his servants could steal it.

He came to Birbal and told him about the robbery. Birbal asked if he suspected anyone. The poor man told that he thought one of his servants had stolen the gold. Birbal called all the 6 servants to him, cut a straw into 6 equal pieces and gave each of them one. Then he mumbled some mantras.

“The straw will grow in the hands of the thief”, he said and continued, “I’m going to check your straws in the morning. You are to stay here for the night.”

The servants were put into separate rooms. The man who had stolen the gold decided to shorten his straw so when it grew, it would be the same size as before. In the morning Birbal checked the length of the straws and at once it became clear who the thief was because his straw was the shortest one. The rich man got his money back.

Questions:

  • 1. Why did the rich man go to Birbal?
  • 2. How did Birbal know who had stolen the gold?
